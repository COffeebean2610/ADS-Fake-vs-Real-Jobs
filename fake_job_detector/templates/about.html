ABOUT THE PROJECT

JobGuard AI

A final-year engineering project built to tackle the growing problem of fraudulent job postings using modern NLP and machine learning.

The Problem

Online job fraud costs job seekers millions of dollars annually and causes significant psychological harm. Fake postings harvest personal data, collect advance fees, or lure applicants into scams. Traditional keyword filters miss sophisticated attacks that use genuine-sounding language.

JobGuard AI addresses this gap by applying Natural Language Processing — the same techniques used by email spam filters — to automatically flag suspicious listings before a job seeker wastes time or shares sensitive information.

System Architecture
  1. Data Ingestion — Kaggle Fake Job Postings dataset (17,880 records, 18 columns).
  2. NLP Preprocessing — Lowercasing, URL removal, punctuation stripping, stop-word filtering (NLTK).
  3. Feature Engineering — Six text columns merged into a single document; TF-IDF with 10k features + bigrams.
  4. Model Training — Logistic Regression and Random Forest evaluated; best saved via joblib.
  5. Serving — Flask REST routes; model loaded once at startup; results returned in <100 ms.
Impact & Use Cases
